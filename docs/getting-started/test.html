Welcome to TestObject! Get ready to create and run your first test in 5 easy steps!
In this tutorial we’ll walk you through each and every step in the making of your first automated GUI testing.

What you need:

  • A web browser
  • An app (*.apk file) (download a sample app here)
  • 5 minutes

Let's get started!

Tired of reading? Check out CONTINUOUS in action!

 


Step 1: Create an account

The CONTINUOUS automated testing solution offers a cloud infrastructure for mobile app testing that is accessible from any web browser. CONTINUOUS allows you to easily upload any app to your own testing account, record tests, add assertions and run those tests on multiple devices. 

In order to start and use CONTINUOUS you first need to create a free account.

Log into TestObject and click “Create an account”.

Fill in your name and Email address, select a username and password and click “I agree. Create my account”

In the following minutes you will receive a registration confirmation E-mail. Once you get it you are good to go!

Step 2: Create a new Project

After you Sign in to your account, Click + New Project to set a new project.

Name your project “MyFirstProject" and click Save.

Choose whether you are going to test a native app or a web app and click accordingly (In this tutorial we will test a native app).

Click Choose APK file and find the App you want to upload to the system.

Name your application “MyApp” and click Save.

Step 3: Record your first test

Click + New Test, name your test “MyFirstTest”, check the Production label, choose a device to record your test with and click Continue.

The Recorder is composed of a toolbar on the left, the device in the middle and a script editor on the right. After the application has loaded on the device, you are ready to start recording. Each action executed by the user will then be added as a line in the script.

In the sample app on the device click Continue and notice that your action has generated a “WELCOME!” message. click Done.


Congratulations! you have successfully recorded a test for your app. Good for you!

If you want to try out some more advanced functionality, read more on Using the Recorder...

Step 4: Add assertions

Now that you have a recorded test, you can easily add assertions. Assertions are statements that can be true or false. Adding an assertion for a specific step helps you discover if a certain occurrence follows an action or not.

Click on the plus icon to open the editor at the specific step you want to add assertions on.

 

On the left side you’ll see two devices side by side which represent your application’s interface before and after your chosen step.

An assertion is created by highlighting the components in your app’s interface that appears or disappears after the chosen step has been executed.

Click the word “AWESOME!” to highlight it on the right device. By highlighting it, you assert that it will appear each time this step is being made.

Click Insert Assertions and you are done.

Step 5: Run your first test

In this step we will run your recorded test automatically. In order to do so, we need to set up a batch test which will define which devices will run our selected tests.
Select your test and click the Play icon on the tests toolbar.
Name your batch “Automated first test”, select the devices you want your test to run on and click Save.
Click the Play icon on the right of your batch to run your test and wait to see if it passed or failed.

Congratulations! You’ve just completed the “5 minutes CONTINUOUS testing for beginners” tutorial.
